Interests

Embedded Devices

Robotics@APEX in middle school and running a maker club in high school exposed me to programming microprocessors and controlling mechanical devices. As the Software Lead at FRUCD, I program PSOCs to manage the transfer of telemetry between embedded devices on the car and a traditional desktop monitoring application. Of particular interest to me are IoT mesh networks. During spring quarter I wrote an engineering paper comparing common IoT protocols to implement an IoT gateway, and more recently at CalHacks I attempted to build a distributed server application on Raspberry Pi's over radio. If I had the chance, I would also like to do research in swarm robotics.

Web Development

Most applications require access to a centralized data system, powered through the internet. I like building web apps because they are universal and simple, and allows me to be creative in the development process. I prefer working with backend services because it allows me to think about how to represent data and how to ensure consistency in its manipulation. As the technical lead for HackDavis, I work on ensuring that our webpage and live-page for the hackathon are well maintained.

Machine Learning

Since I began programming by making a game of pong, I've always been intrigued by Artificial Intelligence. Being able to simulate logic in a way that isn't a string of if statements is what excites me about machine learning algorithms.
